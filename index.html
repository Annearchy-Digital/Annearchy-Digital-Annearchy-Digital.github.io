Man & Machine Learning

"Are you trying to make the robots takeover or save us from the robots?"

This question was the simple driver for my TEDx talk "Man & Machine Learning", which explored the choices we're already giving machines and how we need to seeking to upgrade the software in our fellow humans rather than fearing what's running in our computers.

Featuring an AI-powered rendition of The Trolley Problem, the talk was conceived while Chief Architect at LiveTiles, the fastest growing tech company in Australia, where I led the US technology team.

Why Annearchy?

Annearchy Digital was named for my three daughters - each of which have the middle name Anne. In this family, we believe in Caring, Sharing, and Learning, which are the best parts of what it means to be human. When considering projects, I will only take them if they make a better world for my daughters.

Erik Ralston

Erik's work with startups has taken products from zero to 8.5 million users and companies from zero to 55 million revenue. Erik holds a Bachelor's in Computer Science from Washington State University. He has over 18 years of education and experience in software including building enterprise apps for the US Department of Energy, international consulting, and making mobile tech for B2B and B2C startups. Most recently, he spent 5 years leading the US products team for the fastest growing tech company in Australia.

Erik's skillset ranges from creative to technical. Primarily a technologist in the Microsoft stack, Erik is also a leader and public speaker, and has helped with the advancement of ideas from enterprise collaboration SaaS platforms to food trucks.
